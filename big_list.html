The Big List of Famous Trans* Programmers

A community-driven, open-source compendium of notable trans* programmers and computer science professionals.

In order to recommend additions, revisions, or raise meta discussions, please open a GitHub issue. If you're interested in becoming a contributor, email us at contact@transprogrammer.org and we'll get back to you as soon as we can.

This article is dedicated to the original poster, an unknown reddit user who submitted the list as a self-post to r/transprogrammer on Saturday, September 13, 2014.

Application Development

Anne Ogborn

pronouns
she/her
born
1959 in Salina, Kansas, U.S.
description
Worked at a number of well-known software companies, including Ashton-Tate, EA, and Apple. Worked on Apple's localization systems for Mac OS 8. Won awards for writing WingZ, the first spreadsheet to let the user draw on it. Active in the SWI-Prolog project and various virtual world projects. Also famous for her trans activism: among other things, she started the first organized resistance against MWMF's transphobic policies.
